Part 1: Apostrophes

Use apostrophes for: Contractions (missing letters: don’t = do not) and Possession (belongs to: Sarah’s book). Avoid apostrophes for plurals.
Examples: It’s raining (It is); the dog’s tail (tail of the dog).

Teacher Tips

  • Highlight its vs it’s.
  • Names ending in s: use James’s consistently.
  • Read aloud to check contractions.

Part 2: Homophones

Homophones sound the same but differ in spelling and meaning. Examples: there / their / they’re, your / you’re, its / it’s.

Spot the Difference

  • there (place), their (belonging), they’re (they are)
  • your (belonging), you’re (you are)
  • its (belonging), it’s (it is)
